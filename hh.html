Visual Metaphors in Assamese Cinema

       

An AI-Powered Semiotic Analysis of Cultural Symbolism

       
           

                Exploring the rich tapestry of visual storytelling in Assamese films through                  cutting-edge machine learning and computer vision techniques. This research bridges                  traditional film semiotics with modern AI to decode cultural metaphors.

Research Methodology

           

                Our analysis employs a multi-modal approach combining state-of-the-art vision-language models                  (CLIP, BLIP) with traditional computer vision techniques. We extract semantic, visual, and                  cultural features from film frames containing metaphorical representations, then apply both                  zero-shot and supervised learning paradigms to understand how AI interprets culturally-specific                  visual metaphors.

Data Collection & Preparation

                   

Curated dataset of 13 unique metaphorical scenes from Assamese films.                      Applied culturally-aware augmentation to balance classes while respecting semantic constraints.

               
           
                        
               
               
                   

Feature Extraction

                   

Multi-modal feature extraction using CLIP (768-dim semantic), ResNet-50 (2048-dim visual),                      YOLO (object detection), and Haralick textures (6-dim texture features).

               
           
                        
               
               
                   

Zero-Shot Classification

                   

Achieved 50.8% overall accuracy. Perfect recognition for universal metaphors (River, Rainbow, Train)                      but complete failure on culturally-specific ones (Dheki, Golden Colour).

               
           
                        
               
               
                   

Ensemble Learning

                   

Random Forest with LOOCV achieved perfect 100% accuracy by combining all features,                      demonstrating the power of multi-modal fusion for cultural understanding.

Semiotic Insights

                
           

Universal vs Cultural Metaphors

           

                Our analysis reveals a stark divide in AI's ability to interpret visual metaphors. The 50.8% Zero-Shot                  accuracy masks a binary reality: perfect recognition of universal symbols versus complete failure on                  culturally-embedded metaphors unique to Assamese cinema.            

                        
               
Case Study 1: The Sound of Dheki Paradox
               

                    The "Sound of Dheki" metaphor achieved 0% Zero-Shot accuracy, consistently misclassified as "Bhauna".                      This reveals a fundamental challenge in AI interpretation:                

               
                       
  • The Dheki (traditional rice husking tool) has no representation in Western datasets
    •                    
  • Visual similarity in traditional attire causes confusion with theatrical performances
    •                    
  • AI maps unfamiliar cultural artifacts to nearest known concepts, erasing specificity
    •                
           
                        
               
Case Study 2: The Mirror Shot Surprise
               

                    Despite being a universal cinematic metaphor, Mirror Shot achieved 0% Zero-Shot accuracy,                      confused with Mental Blockage. This unexpected failure suggests:                

               
                       
  • Context matters more than universal symbols in specific cultural narratives
    •                    
  • Assamese cinema may use mirror shots differently than Western conventions
    •                    
  • The ensemble model's perfect correction shows the value of multi-modal analysis
    •                
           
           
               
Success Story: Perfect Ensemble Performance
               

                    The ensemble model's 100% accuracy demonstrates that cultural understanding is possible through AI,                      but requires:                

               
                       
  • Multi-modal fusion: Combining semantic, visual, and object features
    •                    
  • Local training: Learning from culturally-specific examples
    •                    
  • Feature diversity: Low-level features (color, texture) provide crucial context
    •                
           
                        

Implications for Film Studies

           

                This research reveals that AI systems trained on Western data exhibit significant cultural bias,                  achieving only 50.8% accuracy on Assamese metaphors. However, the perfect performance of ensemble                  models shows that culturally-aware AI is achievable through appropriate training and multi-modal approaches.
